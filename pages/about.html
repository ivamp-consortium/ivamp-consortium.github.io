The Initiative for Venom Associated Microbes and Parasites (iVAMP) is a collaborative, open-source group of researchers worldwide of all career stages and types with the shared interest to expand the reach of each otherâ€™s work as well as the direction the host-microbe interaction, microbiology, and venomics fields.

What is iVAMP's long-term aims?

1) To study venom glands as potential microenvironments for microorganisms.
2) To establish an inclusive network of scientists interested in microbe- and parasite- venom interactions.
3) To be a platform for sharing of samples to facilitate access to geographically restricted specimens.
4) To support the sharing of data through collaboration, and for obtaining a broader view of the research area.
5) To culminate data from collaborations as an open-source database, free to the public.
6) To engage the general public with ongoing research projects through continuous inquiry and discussion.
